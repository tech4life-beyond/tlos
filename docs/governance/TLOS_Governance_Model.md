# Tech4Life Operating System (TLOS)
## Governance Model

---

## 1. Governance Philosophy

Tech4Life & Beyond operates under a **horizontal governance model**.

Governance is not a mechanism of control, but a system of coordination, protection, and ethical alignment.

There are no hierarchical authorities. There are **facilitators, stewards, and guardians of purpose**.

Power is not accumulated. Responsibility is shared.

---

## 2. Leadership as Stewardship

Leadership within Tech4Life & Beyond is defined as **stewardship**.

A steward:
- Serves the system and its participants
- Protects ethical coherence
- Facilitates collaboration
- Prevents concentration of power

Stewards do not command. They enable.

Roles are functional, temporary, and revocable.

---

## 3. Decision-Making Framework

### 3.1 Default: Consent-Based Decision Making

Most decisions are made using **consent**, not unanimity.

A decision is approved when:
- There are no reasoned objections based on ethics, safety, or systemic harm
- Participants have had adequate time and information

Silence is not consent. Participation is encouraged, but not coerced.

---

### 3.2 Consensus for High-Impact Decisions

Decisions that significantly affect:
- Ethical direction
- Governance structure
- Long-term strategy

Require **consensus** among the relevant contributors.

If consensus cannot be reached, the decision is paused, not forced.

---

## 4. Ethical Veto

Any participant, regardless of level, may invoke an **Ethical Veto**.

An Ethical Veto may be used when a proposal:
- Causes harm to humans
- Damages the environment
- Enables exploitation or manipulation
- Contradicts the Foundation principles

The Ethical Veto does not cancel the proposal permanently.

It triggers:
- Mandatory review
- Ethical analysis
- Collective reflection

Ethical concerns always override speed or profit.

---

## 5. Conflict Resolution

Conflict is treated as a signal, not a failure.

The resolution process follows these steps:

1. Direct dialogue between involved parties
2. Mediation by a neutral steward
3. Collective reflection with affected contributors

No conflict is resolved through authority or coercion.

---

## 6. Role Rotation and Power Distribution

To prevent power concentration:
- Facilitation roles rotate regularly
- No individual may permanently occupy a coordination role
- Knowledge is documented and shared

If a role becomes a source of influence rather than service, it must be redesigned or dissolved.

---

## 7. Transparency and Documentation

All governance decisions must be:
- Documented
- Accessible to relevant participants
- Traceable over time

Transparency is the default.

Exceptions are limited to:
- Legal obligations
- Personal privacy
- Protection of the core system

---

## 8. Accountability Without Punishment

Accountability is restorative, not punitive.

When misalignment occurs:
- The focus is on learning and correction
- Harm is acknowledged and addressed
- Repeated ethical violations may result in removal from participation

Removal is not punishment. It is protection of the system.

---

## 9. Founder Role (Controlled Exception)

The founder does not hold permanent unilateral authority.

The founder acts as:
- Guardian of purpose
- Custodian of the Foundation
- Steward of long-term coherence

### 9.1 When intervention is allowed (Triggers)

The founder may intervene only when at least one of the following is true:
- Core principles are at risk
- Ethical integrity is threatened
- The system is being distorted in a way that endangers mission, safety, or enforceability
- A time-critical safety or legal risk requires immediate containment

### 9.2 Required procedure (Minimum Controls)

Any founder intervention must include:
1. **Written justification** (what happened, why it triggers intervention)
2. **Scope** (what is being paused/overridden and for how long)
3. **Impact statement** (who is affected)
4. **A record** created in GitHub (issue or ADR reference) to preserve traceability

### 9.3 Review and sunset

- The intervention must be reviewed by relevant stewards/architects as soon as practical.
- If no review occurs, the intervention must expire (“sunset”) and revert to the last stable governance state.

### 9.4 Appeal path

Any affected participant may request an appeal.
Appeals must:
- be documented
- include a reasoned objection (ethics/safety/system harm)
- be reviewed under consent/consensus rules depending on impact level

---

## 10. Living Governance

This governance model is a living system.

It evolves through:
- Experience
- Reflection
- Ethical learning

Any evolution must preserve:
- Horizontal leadership
- Ethical primacy
- Human dignity

---

**Governance exists to protect creation, not to dominate it.**
